Full Federal Court of Australia Denies ASIC Appeal in Case Against Commonwealth Bank and Colonial, Provides Clarity on Conflicted Remuneration Laws
Pacific - Australia
Australian Securities & Investments Commission
Thursday, August 17, 2023
The Full Federal Court in Australia has concluded to reject the Australian Securities andInvestments Commission (ASIC)'s appeal against a prior judgement dismissing ASICs case against the Commonwealth Bank of Australia (CBA) and Colonial First State: Investments Limited (Colonial). The case revolved around an allegation from ASIC stating that CBA and Colonial contravened conflicted remuneration laws through an agreement that Colonial will pay CBA in order to distribute its Essential Super product via CBA's physical and digital channels. ASIC Deputy Chair, Sarah Court, noted that the Full Court accepted several of ASIC's arguments despite dismissing the appeal, thereby clarifying the interpretation of conflicted remuneration regulations for future matters. ASIC now has 28 days to apply for special leave to appeal to the High Court. The case between CBA and Colonial originated from the Royal Commission's investigation into misconduct in the banking, superannuation, and financial services industry.
